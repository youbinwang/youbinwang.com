## Character and Narrative Design

Set in the fictional **Letter Town**, players control "Hun" — a son of Hades trained as a top swordsman — to uncover the truth behind a town engulfed in illusions. I authored the 17-page narrative script, designed the character arcs and dialogue logic for 5 key NPCs, and tightly coupled story progression with scene unlocks via quest triggers.

| Character | Role | Design Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Hun** | Son of Hades, top swordsman | Player character — fast, forceful combat style |
| **Li An** | Hidden illusionist family, born with forgetfulness | Key clue carrier, triggers the central quest line |
| **Lv Chuan (Scholar)** | Official family but prefers novel writing | Quest giver, drives the main narrative |
| **General Xia** | Great court general, now trapped by illusions | Pre-boss NPC, reveals world background |
| **Cui Yun (Maid)** | Lv Chuan's maid | Supporting NPC, delivers clue information |

<TextImage image="/images/games/the-scholars-side-quest/features/story-plot-narrative.png" alt="Story structure and narrative flow" align="right">

### Narrative Structure

The quest follows a **three-act structure**: Act I "Arrive & Investigate" (gather clues, meet characters), Act II "Break the Illusion" (untangle the NPC curse chains), Act III "Reveal the Truth" (boss fight + ending cutscene). Each dialogue sequence automatically triggers the corresponding animation cutscene, maintaining narrative momentum.

</TextImage>

## Quest Flow Design

<TextImage image="/images/games/the-scholars-side-quest/features/gameplay-dialogue-1.png" alt="Dialogue system screenshot" align="left">

### Dialogue-Driven Progression

The game uses a **dialogue plugin** to power core quest functionality: players must interact with NPCs to advance the quest, and cutscenes trigger automatically at the end of each dialogue sequence. This design merges narrative delivery with quest state updates into a single interaction node, minimizing UI interruption.

</TextImage>

<TextImage image="/images/games/the-scholars-side-quest/features/gameplay-dialogue-2.png" alt="Quest flow and gameplay integration" align="right">

### Collectible Design

A side quest spawned from Li An's forgetfulness: **8 letters hidden throughout Letter Town** — in building corners, bamboo groves, and mansion chambers. Collecting each letter triggers a memory-flashback dialogue fragment that assembles Li An's complete backstory. This mechanic ties exploration gameplay to narrative delivery, giving map traversal emotional stakes.

</TextImage>

## Level Design

The primary setting, **Wang Lian Mansion**, is the game's core zone. I was responsible for the full layout planning and iterative level design — from reference gathering and prototype blocking to final lighting polish.

<TextImage image="/images/games/the-scholars-side-quest/features/map-reference.png" alt="Reference collection for level design" align="left">

### Reference and Concept Phase

Gathered extensive references from traditional Chinese architecture and garden design, then applied stylized abstraction to fit the illusion-world theme: preserving wooden structural elements while adding surreal lighting and particle effects to create an atmosphere where reality and illusion coexist.

</TextImage>

### Bamboo Forest Lighting

The bamboo forest area uses a **Strong Contrasting Color Lighting** strategy: cool blue ambient light against warm orange ground light. This creates a dream-like illusion atmosphere while also helping players distinguish directional paths — functional art direction.

## Animation Production

<TextImage image="/images/games/the-scholars-side-quest/features/rigging.png" alt="Character rigging" align="left">

### Character Rigging Pipeline

I participated in the rigging of key characters — using **Maya** for skeleton construction and weight painting, adjusting skin deformation in Blender, then importing into Unreal Engine 4 for animation integration.

</TextImage>

### 13 Animation Cutscenes

Produced 13 cutscene animations using UE4's **Sequencer** non-linear editing tool. Each cutscene is bound to a specific quest node — key narrative moments (first entry into Wang Lian Mansion, clue discovery, final revelation) all have dedicated animations to amplify emotional impact.

## Combat Design

<TextImage image="/images/games/the-scholars-side-quest/features/combat-01.png" alt="Combat screenshot" align="right">

### Combat Philosophy

As a side quest, the combat system is intentionally **lightweight**: short combo chains and dodge rolls that serve quest progression rather than competitive depth. My responsibilities included setting enemy counts per area, spawn point placement, and combat zone boundaries — ensuring that combat pacing reinforced rather than disrupted the narrative rhythm.

</TextImage>
